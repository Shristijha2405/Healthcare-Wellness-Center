 🏥 Healthcare Wellness Center Dashboard

 📌 Project Overview

The Healthcare Wellness Center Dashboard is an interactive Power BI solution designed to help hospital administrators, healthcare analysts, and decision-makers monitor patient demographics, medical conditions, insurance coverage, and financial performance in a single, centralized view.

The dashboard transforms raw healthcare data into meaningful insights, enabling healthcare organizations to make data-driven decisions, improve operational efficiency, and optimize revenue management.

---

 🎯 Project Objectives

This dashboard aims to:

* Monitor patient and billing information in real time.
* Analyze healthcare revenue across medical conditions, hospitals, doctors, and insurance providers.
* Identify top-performing hospitals and healthcare professionals.
* Understand patient demographics and admission patterns.
* Support strategic decision-making through data visualization.

💡 Key Insights & Findings

 🦴 Arthritis Leads Healthcare Demand

* Arthritis emerged as the most common medical condition.
* It recorded both the highest patient count and the largest billing contribution among all conditions.

 💰 Significant Revenue Variation Across Conditions

* The average billing per medical condition stands at approximately **$236.24M**.
* Certain medical conditions contribute disproportionately to total hospital revenue.

 ⚖️ Balanced Gender Distribution

* Male and female patient populations are nearly equal.
* Both genders contribute similarly to overall admissions and healthcare utilization.

🏦 Insurance Provider Performance

* **Cigna** generated the highest total billing amount.
* Other major contributors include Medicare and Blue Cross, with only marginal differences in revenue contribution.

 👨‍⚕️ Top Revenue-Generating Doctors

* **Michael Smith** ranks as the highest billing doctor.
* **Robert Smith** and **John Smith** follow closely, indicating strong performance among leading healthcare professionals.

 📉 Billing Trend Analysis

* Hospital billing remained relatively stable from **2020 to 2023**.
* A noticeable decline appears in **2024**, potentially due to reduced admissions, operational changes, or cost-control measures.

 🚨 Admission Pattern Insights

* Admission types show an almost equal gender split.
* Female patients account for a slightly higher proportion of urgent admissions.

 🏥 High-Performing Hospitals

* **Johnson PLC** and **Smith PLC** dominate total billing contributions.
* Revenue concentration suggests these hospitals handle a significant share of high-value treatments and patient services.
